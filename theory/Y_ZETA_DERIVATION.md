# Derivation of the y_zeta prediction (Delta_B at the MIPT), QJ Case B

Canonical chain from the microscopic model to the prediction Delta_B(lambda_c) ~ 1.1-1.4,
and the single measurement that closes it.  Epistemic tags: [V]=verified in-project,
[L]=literature-anchored, [P]=plausible/data-consistent, [O]=open.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 0. Model and conventions [V]

1D Kitaev chain at the topological point mu=0, Delta=w, Majorana representation
gamma_a (a=0..2L-1), {gamma_a,gamma_b}=2 delta_ab.  Control lambda = alpha,
hopping w, with alpha+w=1.

Measured operator (rate alpha): the distance-3 Bogoliubov bond
    B_j = i gamma_{2j} gamma_{2j+3},     B_j^2 = 1,
projector P_j^pm = (1/2)(1 +/- B_j).  Unraveling: quantum jumps (clicks) with
Born statistics; between clicks the state follows the non-Hermitian no-click
drift generated by h_eff = H - i alpha sum_j (bond term).  Partial post-selection
(PPS): a trajectory with N_clicks clicks is reweighted by zeta^{N_clicks},
zeta in (0,1]; zeta=1 is the bare Born ensemble.

Gaussian description: Majorana covariance Gamma_{ab} = (i/2)<[gamma_a,gamma_b]>,
so for a != b,  <gamma_a gamma_b> = -i Gamma_ab,  and  <B_j>_Q = Gamma_{2j,2j+3}.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 1. Averaging, Choi doubling, replicas [V/L]

Entanglement and operator-content observables are NONLINEAR in the trajectory
state rho_m (e.g. S = -Tr rho log rho, or <O>^2).  Their Born-rule average
    E[X] = sum_m p_m X(rho_m),   p_m = Tr(K_m rho_0 K_m^dagger),
is computed with R replicas; the constraint sum_m p_m = 1 (probability
normalization) is enforced by taking R -> 1 at the end [Jian-You-Vasseur-Ludwig;
Bao-Choi-Altman].  Each replica is Keldysh/Choi-doubled into a ket (+) and a
bra (-) contour: rho lives in H (x) H*, and a local operator B appears as B_+
(ket) or B_- (bra).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 2. Where zeta enters: the exact recycling expansion [V]

The dephasing measurement channel for an involutive observable (B^2=1) is, on the
doubled state, generated by the "recycling" map B_+ B_- together with a no-click
counterterm.  PPS weights a click by zeta, so the recycling term carries zeta.
Using P^pm = (1/2)(1 +/- B), the ket-bra projector product is exactly

    P_+ P_- = (1/4) ( 1 + B_+ + B_- + B_+ B_- ).            (2.1)

Collecting the zeta-weighted recycling term against the no-click counterterm, the
measurement part of the doubled generator contains [V, Sec. 6 of CURRENT_THEORY_STATUS]

    single-copy mass  (B_+ + B_-)  with coefficient   (alpha/4)(zeta - 1),     (2.2)
    cross vertex      (B_+ B_-)     with coefficient    (alpha/4) zeta.          (2.3)

KEY: the single-copy coefficient is proportional to (zeta-1); it VANISHES at zeta=1
(Born) and turns on for zeta<1.  zeta enters ONLY through these two terms; this is
exact and structural.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 3. Operator content at the free (no-click) anchor [V]

Because <B>_Q != 0 at criticality, the raw cross operator splits:
    B_+ B_- = <B>^2 + <B>(dB_+ + dB_-) + :B_+ B_-:,          (3.1)
i.e. a constant, a dimension-1 single-copy admixture, and the genuine normal-ordered
cross vertex :B_+B_-:.  Measured at the NO-CLICK critical point (deterministic gapless
h_eff = two non-Hermitian SSH chains), with the steady-state covariance:

  * single-copy mass M_sc = B_+ + B_-:  connected 2-pt  C(r) = <B_0 B_r>_c ~ r^{-2.018}
        =>  Delta_B = 1.009 ~ 1.            (relevant)                          (3.2)
  * cross vertex :B_+B_-: :  <:B_+B_-:(r) :B_+B_-:(0)> = C(r)^2 ~ r^{-4.036}
        =>  Delta_{cross} = 2.02 ~ 2.       (marginal)                          (3.3)

One-loop on the replicated PCM coupling g: beta_g ~ -(2n-2) g^2, which vanishes at
the von Neumann limit n -> 1, so the cross vertex is one-loop marginal at n=1 [P].

CRUCIAL CAVEAT: (3.2)-(3.3) are dimensions at the NO-CLICK (free, UV) critical point,
NOT at the MIPT fixed point (strong coupling lambda ~ O(1)).  They are bare/UV values.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 4. Scaling dimension -> RG eigenvalue (d = 2) [V]

The monitored 1+1-D circuit has d = 2 spacetime dimensions.  A perturbation
integral d^2x  g O  with O of scaling dimension Delta_O has coupling RG eigenvalue
    y_O = d - Delta_O = 2 - Delta_O.                          (4.1)
Apply to the two zeta-bearing operators:
  * single-copy mass (couples to g_sc ~ (zeta-1), dimension Delta_B):
        y_zeta = 2 - Delta_B.                                 (4.2)
  * cross vertex (dimension ~2):  y_cross = 2 - 2 = 0  (marginal): contributes NO
        relevant zeta-eigenvalue.                             (4.3)
Hence the relevant zeta-direction is carried by the single-copy mass, and y_zeta is
fixed by Delta_B (at the MIPT, per Sec. 8).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 5. Born = n->1; PPS keeps the whole line at n->1 [P, D8]

The replica limit is set by the trajectory WEIGHTING.  Forced measurement uses equal
weights = n->0 (Anderson/Fulga class).  Born uses Born weights p_m = n->1.  PPS
reweights the Born ensemble, p_m -> p_m zeta^{N_clicks}: a TILT of the Born weights,
NOT a switch to equal weights.  Therefore the entire PPS line zeta in (0,1] stays in
n->1; field-theoretically "zeta enters only the cross vertex" (Sec. 2) = zeta modifies
a vertex inside the n->1 theory; it does not change the replica limit.  Consequences:
the TRANSVERSE exponent (nu, Sec. 6) is fixed along the line; the forced n->0 value is
off the line; zeta=0 is singular (only the no-click trajectory survives, replica trick
degenerates).  Contrast QSD (LMR): there the cross vertex is RELEVANT -> a genuine
zeta* within n->1; the QJ cross vertex is MARGINAL -> no zeta*, nu fixed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 6. Universality class and the transverse exponent y_lambda [L]

No conserved charge (only fermion parity) => symmetry class DIII.  Effective theory:
SO(R) principal chiral model, R -> 1 [Jian-Shapourian-Bauer-Ludwig 2302.09094;
Fava et al.].  The Born transition is finite-nu (NOT KT), driven by Z_2 (vortex)
defect proliferation; Jian's numerics give nu ~ 2.1.

Foster-Guo-Jian-Ludwig (2510.23706) provide the eps = 2 - R expansion (a la Fu-Kane):
anchor at R=2, where SO(R) reduces to one compact boson and the action is sine-Gordon
    S = integral d^2x [ (K/2)(grad phi)^2 - (M/32) cos 4phi ],   K = lambda/4,
with cos4phi (Delta_M = 4/piK) and the dual vortex (Delta_V = piK) BOTH marginal at the
KT point K_c = 2/pi (Delta_M=Delta_V=2).  Deforming R = 2 - eps to the physical MIPT
R -> 1 (eps=1), the noninteracting fixed point has a single relevant direction = the
stiffness = the measurement rate lambda, with RG eigenvalues
    { 1 +/- sqrt(33 - 8x), 2(5 - 2x) } (eps/8),   q = 8 - x eps.
The single positive eigenvalue gives
    y_lambda = (1 + sqrt(33 - 8x)) (eps/8),   nu = 1/y_lambda = (1 + sqrt(33-8x))^{-1}(8/eps).
Matching to Jian's nu ~ 2.1 fixes x = 3 (then at eps=1: sqrt(33-24)=3, y_lambda = 4/8 = 1/2,
nu = 2).  So
    y_lambda ~ 1/2,  nu ~ 2.                                  (6.1)
This RESOLVES the project's y_lambda = 1-vs-1/2 ambiguity: the single-particle no-click
length xi_nc ~ lambda^{-1} (which suggested nu=1) is NOT the many-body correlation length;
the many-body transverse exponent is (6.1).  [Caveat: x=3 is fitted to Jian's numerical nu,
so y_lambda=1/2 is the numerical class-DIII value with an RG scaffolding, not an independent
first-principles number.]  Also: Foster's microscopic model is a DETERMINISTIC-Hamiltonian
monitored Kitaev superconductor (randomness only from measurement) = Case B's setup type,
which nearly removes the random-vs-deterministic caveat in identifying Case B with this class.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 7. The boundary (crossover) exponent phi [P]

For zeta<1 there are TWO relevant scaling fields at the zeta=1 corner:
  * the thermal field  t ~ (lambda_c - lambda),  eigenvalue y_lambda  (Sec. 6),
  * the PPS field      h ~ (1 - zeta),            eigenvalue y_zeta    (Sec. 4).
Under RG, t -> t b^{y_lambda}, h -> h b^{y_zeta}.  The phase boundary is the locus where
the relevant flows balance; eliminating the rescaling b gives the crossover law
    lambda_c(zeta)  ~  (zeta-field)^{ y_lambda / y_zeta },     phi = y_lambda / y_zeta.   (7.1)
With y_lambda = 1/2 this is the relation used in analysis/extract_yzeta.py,
    phi = 1 / (2 y_zeta),                                      (7.2)
and the two-parameter FSS form lambda_c(L,zeta) * sqrt(L) = F( zeta * L^{y_zeta} ) (the
sqrt(L) = L^{-y_lambda} crossing drift).  So with y_lambda fixed (Sec. 6), the ONLY
unknown in the boundary exponent is y_zeta.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 8. The crux: UV (no-click) vs IR (MIPT) dimension [O -> measurable]

Sec. 3 measured Delta_B ~ 1 at the NO-CLICK critical point (free CFT).  The y_zeta that
enters (7.1) is the one at the MIPT fixed point (strong coupling lambda~O(1)):
    y_zeta = 2 - Delta_B(lambda_c),   Delta_B(lambda_c) = the IR dimension.   (8.1)
Delta_B(lambda_c) need NOT equal the no-click value 1; whether it does is exactly the
open "Delta_zeta^IR" question.  This is the single quantity the measurement targets.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 9. The prediction [P]

Combine (6.1), (7.1) and the boundary data (free-exponent fits give phi ~ 0.56 on
lambda_c, ~0.7-0.85 on the physical ratio r_c = lambda_c/(1-lambda_c)):
    y_zeta = y_lambda / phi = (1/2) / phi
           ~ 0.5/0.56 ~ 0.89   (lambda_c parametrization),
           ~ 0.5/0.80 ~ 0.63   (r_c parametrization).
So
    y_zeta ~ 0.6 - 0.9     (relevant, but renormalized below the no-click value 1),  (9.1)
    Delta_B(lambda_c) = 2 - y_zeta ~ 1.1 - 1.4.                                       (9.2)
PREDICTION (9.2): the bond-operator dimension at the MIPT is modestly ABOVE its no-click
value (~1.0).  This is NOT clean sqrt(zeta) (phi=1/2 would need y_zeta=1 exactly) nor
linear (phi=1 needs y_zeta=1/2); it is an intermediate boundary exponent set by (9.2).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
## 10. The measurement that determines y_zeta [the run]

At zeta=1 (Born; NO cloning) sample Born-rule QJ trajectories; from each final-time
covariance Gamma record the bond expectation b_x = Gamma_{2x,2x+3} = <B_x>_Q.  The
MIPT-relevant (nonlinear-in-rho) single-copy-mass correlator is the trajectory COVARIANCE
    C_sc(r) = Cov_traj( <B_x>_Q, <B_{x+r}>_Q )  ~  r^{-2 Delta_B},               (10.1)
fitted (bulk-windowed, translation-averaged) to give Delta_B(lambda_c), hence
y_zeta = 2 - Delta_B and phi = 1/(2 y_zeta).  [Why the covariance, not <B_x B_y>_Q: the
ket-bra (cross-contour) piece of M_sc^2 is E_traj[<B_x>_Q <B_y>_Q], whose connected part
is exactly C_sc; the same-contour piece is linear-in-rho and trivial at long times.]
Run at a small lambda grid bracketing lambda_c(1) ~ 0.5; the lambda with the cleanest
power law is lambda_c, and Delta_B there is the IR dimension.  Independent cross-check:
the boundary-collapse y_zeta from extract_yzeta.py should agree with 2 - Delta_B(lambda_c).
From the same trajectories: X1, X_typ, x2 (squared Majorana correlator -> Case B vs Jian's
class) and c_ent (S(L/2) vs log L).  Code: pps_qj/parallel/worker_opdim_pps.py,
slurm/submit_opdim.sh, analysis/fit_opdim.py.

WHAT STAYS OPEN: the first-principles y_zeta (identify the SO(R)_q primary of the
single-copy mass and track it to R->1) is obstructed by the change of anchor between the
project's free-fermion point (K0=1) and Foster's R=2 boson (K_c=2/pi) -- a Meidan-scale
calculation.  The measurement (10.1) sidesteps it.
