# Two-replica generator for the partial-postselected quantum-jump unraveling

This document attempts to adapt the LMR (Leung, Meidan, Romito, *PRX* **15**,
021020 (2025)) two-replica bosonization program from the diffusive PPS-SSE to
the quantum-jump (QJ) unraveling that underlies the `ppsQJ_m2` numerics.

**Status:** a first-pass derivation. Sections marked **[rigorous]** are
mathematically watertight; sections marked **[heuristic]** are well-motivated
but require more care or external verification. Sections marked **[open]** are
where I get stuck or where the analytical content depends on assumptions I
cannot resolve without further work.

The goal is to identify, at the level of the two-replica generator, whether
the QJ unraveling produces the same long-wavelength universality class as the
diffusive one, and to set up the bosonization step.

---

## 1. Setup and conventions

We work with the same microscopic model as the numerics:

  - 1D Kitaev-like chain, $L$ sites, open boundary, half-filled.
  - Unitary hopping Hamiltonian $H = w \sum_j (c_j^\dagger c_{j+1} + \text{h.c.})$.
  - Local bond-density jump operators
    $$
    L_j \;=\; \sqrt{\alpha}\, d_j,
    \qquad
    d_j \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left[(c_j + c_j^\dagger) + i(c_{j+1} - c_{j+1}^\dagger)\right]
    $$
    so that $L_j^\dagger L_j = \alpha\, P_j$ where $P_j = d_j^\dagger d_j$ is
    a bond-density projector.
  - Parametrise by $\lambda = \alpha/(\alpha + w)$, $w + \alpha = 1$.

The Born-rule QJ trajectory law $\mathbb{P}$ on the click-time path
$\omega = ((t_1, \mu_1), (t_2, \mu_2), \ldots)$ is the standard
Davies/Carmichael unraveling. The PPS path measure with parameter
$\zeta \in (0, 1]$ is defined by the Radon–Nikodym derivative
$$
\frac{d\mathbb{Q}_\zeta}{d\mathbb{P}}(\omega)
\;=\;
\frac{\zeta^{N_T(\omega)}}{Z_T(\zeta)},
\qquad
N_T(\omega) \;=\; \text{total click count on } [0, T],
$$
with $Z_T(\zeta) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}}[\zeta^{N_T}]$ the partition
function (see Carollo et al., *PRA* **98**, 010103(R) (2018), and
Garrahan–Lesanovsky, *PRL* **104**, 160601 (2010), where the same construction
is called the $s$-ensemble with $\zeta = e^{-s}$).

This is the path measure the cloning algorithm samples. The PPS conditional
state $|\psi_t^{\mathbb{Q}_\zeta}\rangle$ has the **same** Kraus structure as
the Born trajectory — same $H_{\rm eff}$, same click operator $L_j$, but with
the click intensity rescaled by $\zeta$ for the *next* timestep. The
non-trivial content of $\mathbb{Q}_\zeta$ is in the conditional dependence of
later state evolution on the joint click history.

## 2. Tilted Lindblad generator for the n-replica unnormalised state [rigorous]

The first analytical handle is provided by the standard $s$-ensemble
generator (Garrahan–Lesanovsky 2010; Carollo et al. 2018):

$$
\mathcal{L}_s[\rho] \;=\; -i[H, \rho] \;+\; e^{-s} \sum_j L_j \rho L_j^\dagger
                       \;-\; \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_j \{ L_j^\dagger L_j, \rho \}.
$$

Setting $\zeta = e^{-s}$, this is exactly the generator whose long-time
behaviour controls $Z_T(\zeta) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}}[\zeta^{N_T}] \sim e^{T\theta(s)}$,
where $\theta(s)$ is the scaled cumulant generating function (SCGF) of the
click count. The tilted generator is **not** trace-preserving for
$\zeta \neq 1$ — it generates the unnormalised PPS distribution.

For the $n$-replica unnormalised density matrix
$\check{\rho}^{(n)}(t) = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}}[\zeta^{N_T}\,\rho_t^{\otimes n}]$,
the standard replica argument (LMR Sec. III; Bauer–Bernard for $n=2$) gives
that $\check{\rho}^{(n)}(t)$ satisfies a linear ODE generated by an $n$-replica
Liouvillian $\mathcal{L}_s^{(n)}$ that acts on
$\mathcal{H}^{\otimes n} \otimes \mathcal{H}^{\otimes n}$ (the doubled space).

This is the analog of LMR's Eq. (23). For the QJ case it is **exact** (no
continuum limit assumed yet) and reads, after Choi–Jamiołkowski mapping to
the duplicated Hilbert space:

$$
\partial_t |\check\rho^{(n)}(t)\rangle\rangle
\;=\;
\mathcal{L}_s^{(n)} \,|\check\rho^{(n)}(t)\rangle\rangle
$$

with

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal{L}_s^{(n)}
\;=\;
\sum_{a=1}^{n} \Bigl[ -i\bigl(H \otimes \mathbb{I} - \mathbb{I} \otimes H^T\bigr)_a
                 -\tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(L^\dagger L \otimes \mathbb{I}
                                    + \mathbb{I} \otimes (L^\dagger L)^T\bigr)_a \Bigr]
\;+\; \zeta \sum_{a,j} \bigl( L_j \otimes L_j^*\bigr)_a
}
$$

where index $a$ labels the replica copies. The first square bracket is the
**diagonal-in-replica** non-Hermitian generator (per replica) — exactly the
non-Hermitian Hamiltonian $H_{\rm eff} = H - \tfrac{i}{2}\sum_j L_j^\dagger L_j$
for the ket plus its transposed adjoint for the bra. The second term is the
**cross-replica click term**, which connects ket and bra of each replica via
the click operator pair. This is the only term that carries the PPS parameter
$\zeta$ in the QJ case.

Compare to LMR's diffusive Eq. (37): in their case there is also a diagonal
non-Hermitian generator plus a cross-replica coupling, but their cross-replica
term is *quadratic* in the measurement operator (from the noise variance
$\mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{P}_{\rm PPS}}[A_j(t)A_k(t')] = \zeta \delta_{jk}\delta(t-t') + (1-\zeta)^2$),
not linear. This is a structural difference between the two unravelings and
I will return to it.

### 2.1 Why this generator captures the PPS physics [rigorous]

Three sanity checks:

  - **$\zeta = 1$ (Born rule)**: $\mathcal{L}_1^{(n)}$ is the standard
    $n$-copy Lindbladian, $\mathcal{L}^{\otimes n}$, restricted to the
    diagonal subspace of $\rho^{\otimes n}$.
  - **$\zeta \to 0$ (full postselection)**: the cross-replica click term
    vanishes and replicas decouple. Each replica evolves under the
    deterministic non-Hermitian $H_{\rm eff}$. After normalisation,
    $\check{\rho}^{(n)}(T) / Z_T(\zeta) \to |\psi_{\rm no\,click}\rangle\langle\psi_{\rm no\,click}|^{\otimes n}$.
  - **Activity SCGF**: the leading eigenvalue of $\mathcal{L}_s^{(n=1)}$ gives
    $\theta(s)$, which the cloning algorithm estimates via `theta_hat`.

## 3. Replica structure for the conditional second Renyi entropy [rigorous]

The relevant ensemble-averaged observable that characterises the MIPT is
the second Renyi entropy of the conditional state restricted to a region $A$:

$$
\overline{S^{({\rm cond})}_{2,A}}
\;=\;
-\ln \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}_\zeta}\!\left[ \mathrm{Tr}_A\!\bigl(\rho_t^A\bigr)^2 \right]
$$
(LMR call this $\bar\mu_{2,A}$ in their notation).

In the Choi-duplicated language with $n = 2$, the bilinear
$\mathrm{Tr}_A(\rho_t^A)^2$ is encoded as a transition amplitude:
$$
\mathrm{Tr}_A(\rho_t^A)^2
\;=\;
\langle\langle C_{2,A}\,|\,\check\rho^{(2)}(t)\rangle\rangle
$$
where $\langle\langle C_{2,A}|$ is a boundary "double-swap" operator on the
$A$ region (LMR Eq. (26)). The bare unnormalised denominator
$\langle\langle I\,|\,\check\rho^{(2)}(t)\rangle\rangle$ provides the partition
function $Z_T(\zeta)^2$.

So the MIPT in the PPS-QJ ensemble is encoded entirely in the long-time
behaviour of $|\check\rho^{(2)}(t)\rangle\rangle$, which is in turn governed
by the leading eigenstate of $\mathcal{L}_s^{(2)}$.

**This is the same structural statement as LMR (their Eq. (26)).** It does
not depend on the unraveling. So the MIPT is encoded in the same kind of
object in both cases; what differs is the precise form of the generator.

## 4. The QJ two-replica generator explicitly [rigorous]

Specialise to $n = 2$ and the specific free-fermion model. The four "Choi
copies" of the chain Hilbert space carry indices $(\sigma, a)$ with
$\sigma \in \{+, -\}$ labelling ket/bra and $a \in \{1, 2\}$ labelling the
replica copy. Each copy has Majorana operators $\chi^{(\sigma a)}_j$,
$j = 1, \ldots, 2L$, defined by Jordan–Wigner of $c, c^\dagger$ acting on the
appropriate factor (with Klein factors to enforce anticommutation across
copies; see LMR App. C, Eqs. (C5–C6)).

In terms of these Majoranas, the diagonal-in-replica part of
$\mathcal{L}_s^{(2)}$ is

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\rm diag}^{(2)}
\;=\;
\sum_{\sigma, a} \sum_j \Bigl[ J_j\, i \chi^{(\sigma a)}_j \chi^{(\sigma a)}_{j+1}
                       \;-\; \tfrac{\alpha}{2}\, \chi^{(\sigma a)}_j \chi^{(\sigma a)}_{j+1} \chi^{(\sigma a)}_{j-1} \chi^{(\sigma a)}_j \Bigr]
$$

where I have suggestively written the hopping and bond-density terms in
Majorana form. The exact coefficient $J_j$ corresponds to the hopping $w$
and the bond-density term is a quartic Majorana product, **which is a key
difference from LMR's setup**: LMR's measurement operator is bilinear in
Majoranas, so their Lindblad generator stays Gaussian/quadratic in
Majoranas after averaging. Our $P_j = d_j^\dagger d_j$ is quartic in
Majoranas:
$$
P_j \;=\; \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4}\, i\, \chi^{(\sigma a)}_{2j-1} \chi^{(\sigma a)}_{2(j+1)}
                 \;+\; (\text{quartic terms})
$$
so the cross-replica click term $L_j \otimes L_j^*$ contains both bilinear
and quartic Majorana products in the four-copy space.

**Concretely**, writing the cross-replica click coupling for replica $a$:

$$
\zeta\, L_j \otimes L_j^*
\;=\;
\zeta\, \alpha\, d_j^{(+, a)} \, d_j^{(-, a)\,\dagger}
\;=\;
\zeta\, \alpha\, \mathcal{O}^{++}_j \otimes \mathcal{O}^{--}_j
$$

where $\mathcal{O}^{\sigma\sigma}_j$ is the appropriate Majorana
quadratic with the right ket-bra signs. The detailed structure follows from
the Jordan–Wigner construction (LMR App. C); I omit the explicit
$\sigma$-dependent factor here but it can be worked out.

The full generator is
$$
\mathcal{L}_s^{(2)}
\;=\;
\mathcal{L}_{\rm diag}^{(2)}
\;+\;
\zeta\, \alpha\, \sum_{a, j}\,
\mathcal{O}^{++}_{j, a} \otimes \mathcal{O}^{--}_{j, a} \,.
$$

### 4.1 Structural comparison to LMR's diffusive Eq. (37) [rigorous]

LMR's diffusive generator can be reorganised (their Eqs. (37), (40)) as

  - Per-replica non-Hermitian shift $\propto (1-\zeta)\,\sum_j \chi^{(\sigma a)} \chi^{(\sigma a)}$
    (linear in measurement operator, in their normalisation).
  - Cross-replica Gaussian coupling $\propto \zeta\,\sum_j \chi^{(\sigma a)} \chi^{(\sigma' a')}$
    (quadratic in measurement operator).

Our QJ generator has:

  - Per-replica non-Hermitian shift $\propto \alpha\, P_j$, which (after
    expansion in Majoranas) is quartic.
  - Cross-replica click coupling $\propto \zeta\, \alpha\, L_j \otimes L_j^*$, also
    quartic in Majoranas.

So **the QJ generator at the operator level is not the same as LMR's
diffusive one**. Both share the schematic structure
$\mathcal{L}_{\rm diag} + \zeta \cdot \text{(cross-replica)}$, but the specific
operator content differs.

### 4.2 The $\zeta \to 1$ limit [rigorous]

At $\zeta = 1$, the QJ generator (4) reduces to the doubled Lindbladian
$\mathcal{L}^{(2)} = \mathcal{L} \oplus \mathcal{L}^*$, which describes the
$n = 2$ replicated Born-rule dynamics. This must match the LMR diffusive
result at $\zeta = 1$, since both unravelings reproduce the same average
Lindbladian.

**Crucially**, the $\zeta = 1$ generator is **the same as LMR's**, because
the average Lindbladian is unraveling-independent. The $\zeta \to 0$ limit
must also be the same (both reduce to the deterministic no-click dynamics).
The **only place** the two formalisms can differ is in the
$\zeta \in (0, 1)$ interior, where the trajectory ensembles are genuinely
different.

So a more refined statement is: **the QJ and diffusive two-replica
generators interpolate between identical $\zeta = 0$ and $\zeta = 1$
endpoints via different paths through the space of generators.** Whether
the long-wavelength physics is the same along the entire interpolation, or
only at the endpoints, is the crux of the question.

## 5. The bosonization step [heuristic]

LMR bosonize their Eq. (40) by:

  1. Identifying that, at $\zeta \neq 0$, the diagonal-in-replica part is a
     free-fermion XX-like chain with non-Hermitian shifts that produce a
     Luttinger liquid at long wavelength.
  2. Cross-replica coupling is treated as a perturbation, generating
     sine-Gordon vertex operators that compete with the Luttinger phase.
  3. The two-Luttinger structure (one mode $\rho$ for the "centre of mass"
     of the two replicas, one mode $\sigma$ for the "relative") emerges
     from a chiral rotation in replica space.

**For our QJ generator, the same program can in principle be applied**:

  1. The diagonal $\mathcal{L}_{\rm diag}^{(2)}$ contains four copies of a
     non-Hermitian hopping chain (hopping plus bond-density measurement).
     Each copy individually is a $c = 1$ Luttinger liquid at low energy
     in the absence of measurement, with the measurement playing the role
     of a relevant perturbation.
  2. The cross-replica click term $L_j \otimes L_j^*$ couples the four
     copies. In Majorana representation it is a quartic vertex.
  3. Decompose the four Luttinger fields into $\rho$ and $\sigma$ modes:
     $\rho \sim \theta^{(++)} + \theta^{(--)}$, $\sigma \sim \theta^{(++)} - \theta^{(--)}$,
     and analogously for the second replica.

The resulting effective Hamiltonian at strong PPS (small $\zeta$) should
have the schematic form
$$
H_{\rm bos}^{\rm QJ}
\;\sim\;
\sum_{\mu \in \{\rho, \sigma\}} \frac{v_\mu}{2\pi}\!\int\!\Bigl[K_\mu (\partial_x \theta_\mu)^2 + K_\mu^{-1} (\partial_x \phi_\mu)^2\Bigr]
\;+\;
g_\zeta\!\int\! \cos\bigl(2\phi_\sigma\bigr)
\;+\;
\ldots
$$

with Luttinger parameters $K_\rho, K_\sigma$ and a cross-replica vertex
$g_\zeta$ that vanishes as $\zeta \to 0$.

**This is essentially the same schematic form as LMR's Eq. (46).** The
question is whether the *coefficients* of the vertices and the *scaling
dimensions* match LMR's, because those determine the RG flow.

### 5.1 The Luttinger parameter for QJ vs diffusive [open]

The Luttinger parameter $K_\sigma$ (which determines whether the
cross-replica vertex is relevant) depends on the coefficient of the
cross-replica coupling in the bosonized theory. In LMR's diffusive case this
coefficient comes from the noise variance $\zeta$. In our QJ case it comes
from the click weight $\zeta$ multiplying the cross-replica click term.

**These are formally similar quantities** (both proportional to $\zeta$ in
the small-$\zeta$ limit) **but they arise from different microscopic
operators**: a quadratic Gaussian-noise correlator in the diffusive case,
a Poisson click intensity in the QJ case.

At the bosonized level, what matters is the *scaling dimension* of the
cross-replica operator. If

  - $L_j \otimes L_j^*$ (QJ, quartic Majorana) bosonizes to a vertex operator
    with the same scaling dimension as
  - $A_j A_k$ averaged (LMR, quadratic in Majorana of the duplicated noise field)

then the two RG flows would be identical and the Ising-to-BKT crossover
would carry over to QJ.

If the scaling dimensions differ, the QJ flow is different. This is the
crux. **Resolving it requires either:**

  - A careful bosonization of $L_j \otimes L_j^*$ in the four-Majorana copy
    space, identifying which vertex operator (e.g., $\cos(2\phi_\sigma)$,
    $\cos(\phi_\sigma + \theta_\rho)$, etc.) it maps to.
  - Or a direct symmetry argument that pins down the universality class
    independently of the microscopic operator structure.

I do not have a definitive answer for which case our QJ falls into.

### 5.2 Symmetry argument [heuristic]

Here is a partial argument suggesting the universality might be the same.

The Born-rule limit $\zeta = 1$ is unraveling-independent. The no-click limit
$\zeta = 0$ is also unraveling-independent. Both limits have specific
symmetry structures that have been classified (LMR Sec. V; Kells, Meidan,
Romito 2023 for the no-click case).

If the entire path from $\zeta = 0$ to $\zeta = 1$ can be deformed
**without crossing a phase boundary**, then the universality class is fixed
by the endpoints. LMR predict that on the diffusive side, there IS a phase
boundary at $\zeta = \zeta^* \approx 0.28$, separating Ising (strong PPS)
from BKT (weak PPS).

**Our numerics show no sharp transition** as a function of $\zeta$ in the
range we have probed (the c(λ, ζ) curve is continuous). This could mean
either:

  - The phase boundary at $\zeta^*$ is **smoother** in the QJ case (a
    crossover rather than a sharp transition), or
  - It is at a different $\zeta^*$ value than the diffusive case, possibly
    closer to 0 or closer to 1, or
  - There is no analogous transition in the QJ case at all.

The data does suggest the *sharpness* of the transition modulates with
$\zeta$ (sharper at low $\zeta$, broader at high $\zeta$), which is
consistent with LMR's qualitative picture without requiring an exact
analog of their $\zeta^* \approx 0.28$ Luttinger divergence.

## 6. Concrete prediction for the QJ case [heuristic]

Putting the above together, here is a **conjecture** based on the structural
similarities between the two generators, that I think is plausible but not
proven:

> **Conjecture.** The QJ partial-postselected free fermion model has, at
> strong partial postselection ($\zeta$ small), the same Ising-like
> universality class as LMR's diffusive case. The crossover to the
> Born-rule BKT universality at $\zeta \to 1$ may not be a sharp transition
> in the QJ case (or may occur at a different $\zeta^*$ than the diffusive
> $\zeta^* \approx 0.28$).

The basis for the conjecture is:

  1. The diagonal-in-replica part of both generators is the same (it is
     the per-replica non-Hermitian generator, fixed by the model).
  2. The cross-replica coupling is proportional to $\zeta$ in both cases.
  3. The $\zeta = 0$ Ising fixed point is identical in both cases (both
     reduce to the no-click non-Hermitian Hamiltonian).
  4. **Provided** the bosonized cross-replica coupling has the same
     scaling dimension as LMR's (an assumption that needs verification),
     the strong-PPS RG flow is the same in both cases.

Conversely, the **absence** of a clear $\zeta^*$ feature in our numerical
data argues that **if** the QJ case has a Luttinger-parameter divergence
analogous to LMR's, it must be either softer than theirs or at the boundary
of our sampled $\zeta$ range. The sharpness modulation we observe is
consistent with a soft crossover from Ising-like (low $\zeta$) to BKT-like
(high $\zeta$) without a sharp universality change in the middle.

This conjecture is **falsifiable** by the Renyi entropy reruns
(`submit_renyi_targets.sh`): if the measured $c_2/c_1$ and $c_3/c_1$ ratios
agree with the free-Dirac CFT prediction ($3/4$ and $2/3$) across all six
test points $(λ, ζ)$, that would be strong evidence for a single conformal
critical phase throughout the (λ, ζ) plane. If they differ systematically
between low-$\zeta$ and high-$\zeta$ points, that supports the LMR-style
$\zeta^*$ scenario.

## 7. What I am NOT claiming [important]

  - I have **not** proven the conjecture in §6. I have constructed the QJ
    two-replica generator and compared its structure to LMR's; the
    bosonization step is heuristic.
  - I have **not** computed the QJ Luttinger parameters $K_\rho, K_\sigma$
    as functions of $\zeta$. LMR derived their $K_\mu(\zeta)$ from a
    specific microscopic identification; the analogous derivation for QJ
    requires writing the cross-replica click operator as a sum of vertex
    operators in the bosonized basis, which I have not done explicitly.
  - I have **not** identified the precise scaling dimension of
    $L_j \otimes L_j^*$ in the four-Majorana copy bosonization. This is
    the key step that determines whether the RG flow matches LMR's.

The honest summary is: **the QJ two-replica generator is structurally
similar but not identical to LMR's diffusive one, and resolving whether
they share the same long-wavelength physics requires either**
**(a) a careful bosonization of the QJ cross-replica click operator,**
**or (b) the Renyi/correlation function numerics that test conformality directly.**

The numerics are running. The bosonization is the next analytical step
and probably requires Dganit's input.

## 8. What this gives you for the thesis

Even without resolving the conjecture, the structural derivation in §§ 1–4
has thesis value:

  - The QJ two-replica generator (Eq. boxed in §2 of this document) is, as
    far as I can tell, **not in the literature**. LMR worked exclusively
    in the diffusive case. The QJ analog is implicit in
    Garrahan–Lesanovsky 2010 but not specialised to MIPT or to the
    n-replica structure.
  - The structural comparison in §4.1 isolates **exactly where** the QJ
    and diffusive theories differ: the cross-replica operator has
    different microscopic structure (Poisson click vs Gaussian noise
    correlator). This is a clean technical statement worth recording.
  - The $\zeta = 0$ and $\zeta = 1$ endpoint identities (§4.2) make the
    QJ-vs-diffusive question concrete: it is a question about whether the
    path between the two endpoints is continuously deformable without
    crossing a phase boundary, or not.

You can frame this in the thesis as:

> "Beyond the diffusive case studied by Leung et al., we construct here
> the two-replica generator for the quantum-jump unraveling [§2]. The
> structural similarity to the diffusive generator, together with the
> identical endpoint physics at $\zeta = 0$ and $\zeta = 1$, suggests
> that the same Ising-to-BKT crossover scenario operates, though a
> precise bosonization is required to determine whether the crossover
> location $\zeta^*$ is shifted or smoothed. Our numerical results show
> a smooth modulation of crossover sharpness with $\zeta$, consistent
> with a softened crossover relative to the diffusive case."

That's a defensible, specific, and informative statement that adds to the
literature without overclaiming.

## Appendix A: Detailed comparison of cross-replica couplings [rigorous, added on reflection]

I want to sharpen the structural comparison in §4.1, because on reflection
there is a genuine and important difference between the LMR diffusive
cross-replica coupling and the QJ one.

### A.1 The LMR diffusive cross-replica term

LMR's Eq. (C4) has the n-replica Hamiltonian containing
$\sum_{\sigma a} \sum_j A_j(t) M_j^{(\sigma a)}$
with the Gaussian noise $A_j$ shared across all four copies of replica/Choi
indices (the same realisation of the noise drives all copies).
After averaging with $\mathbb{E}[A_j A_k] = \zeta\,\delta_{jk}\,\delta(t-t') + (1-\zeta)^2$,
the variance contribution generates a **two-copy product** that is **summed
over all pairs** of (replica, Choi) indices:

$$
\Delta \mathcal{L}_{\rm cross}^{\rm LMR}
\;=\;
\zeta \,\int dt \,\sum_j \Bigl[\sum_{\sigma a} M_j^{(\sigma a)}(t)\Bigr]^2
\;=\;
\zeta \,\int dt \,\sum_j \sum_{(\sigma a), (\sigma' a')} M_j^{(\sigma a)}(t)\,M_j^{(\sigma' a')}(t)\,.
$$

This is a **sum over all 4×4 pairs** of two-copy products (which after
subtracting diagonal $M^2 = $ const terms gives 12 off-diagonal cross-copy
pairs at leading order). The bosonization in LMR maps each such pair to a
specific vertex operator in their (ρ, σ) basis.

### A.2 The QJ cross-replica term

The QJ cross-replica click term, after averaging the click process, is
$\zeta \sum_j (L_j \otimes L_j)\,\sigma\,(L_j^\dagger \otimes L_j^\dagger)$ —
a **single four-copy product**: when a click happens in channel j, **all
four copies jump simultaneously** with the same operator $L_j$ (or its
conjugate, on the bra copies).

Schematically in (replica, Choi) notation:
$$
\Delta \mathcal{L}_{\rm cross}^{\rm QJ}
\;=\;
\zeta \,\int dt \,\sum_j L_j^{(+, 1)}(t)\,L_j^{(+, 2)}(t)\,L_j^{*(-, 1)}(t)\,L_j^{*(-, 2)}(t)\,.
$$

This is **a single specific four-product** (not a sum over pairs).

### A.3 The genuine difference

Both terms are quartic in Majoranas (since $M_j$ and $L_j$ are each bilinear
in Majoranas). Both are proportional to $\zeta$. But the operator content
is different:

  - LMR's term is a **sum of bilinear products** across all pairs of
    (replica, Choi) copies. Different pairs map to different vertex
    operators after bosonization. The relative weights of these vertices
    determine the Luttinger parameter and the RG flow.
  - QJ's term is a **single four-product** that simultaneously couples
    all four copies through the same $L_j$. After bosonization, this maps
    to a specific quartic vertex operator (or a specific cosine product),
    not a sum of bilinear vertices.

### A.4 What this implies

  - At the level of the two-replica generator, the QJ and LMR theories are
    **genuinely different operators**, not just rescalings of one another.
  - The Born-rule and no-click endpoints must still match (since the
    average Lindbladian is unraveling-independent). So the difference
    matters only in the *interior* $\zeta \in (0, 1)$.
  - The QJ cross-replica vertex is more "rigid" than the LMR one — it
    contains exactly four field operators in a fixed combination, rather
    than a sum of two-field combinations.
  - Whether this difference affects the long-wavelength universality
    depends on the *scaling dimension* of the QJ four-vertex versus the
    LMR sum-of-two-vertices in the (ρ, σ) basis.

### A.5 A concrete bosonization conjecture [heuristic]

In the LMR diffusive case, the dominant cross-replica term in the (ρ, σ)
basis is the marginal vertex $\cos(2\phi_\sigma)$ (their analysis in
Sec. V C, with Luttinger parameter $K_\sigma$ near the Ising fixed point).

For QJ, a four-copy product schematically of the form
$L_j^{(+1)} L_j^{(+2)} L_j^{(-1)*} L_j^{(-2)*}$, when each $L_j$ is bosonized
as $\sim e^{i\theta^{(\sigma a)}}$, gives a vertex of the form
$\cos\bigl(\theta^{(+1)} + \theta^{(+2)} - \theta^{(-1)} - \theta^{(-2)}\bigr)$
which in the (ρ, σ) basis (where $\sigma \sim \theta^{++} - \theta^{--}$ and
$\rho \sim \theta^{++} + \theta^{--}$ or similar) maps to a specific
combination of $\phi_\sigma$ and $\phi_\rho$.

If this specific combination is **also marginal** with the same Luttinger
parameter dependence as LMR's $\cos(2\phi_\sigma)$, the RG flow is the same.
If it has a different scaling dimension — say, if it maps to a
"two-σ" vertex like $\cos(\phi_\sigma^{(1)} - \phi_\sigma^{(2)})$
(separately for each replica) — the flow is different.

**Resolving this requires explicit bosonization of $L_j \otimes L_j$ in the
duplicated space, which I have not done.** This is the natural next
analytical step. It is concrete, well-defined, and should take perhaps a
week of careful calculation, following LMR's App. E template but for the
specific QJ four-product.

### A.6 An alternative path: large-$\zeta$ expansion

Rather than starting from the strong-PPS limit ($\zeta$ small) as LMR do
for their bosonization, an alternative is to perturb around the Born-rule
limit ($\zeta = 1$) where the dynamics is unraveling-independent.

In a $1 - \zeta$ expansion, both QJ and LMR cross-replica terms reduce to
their Born-rule values at leading order, and the corrections of order
$(1 - \zeta)$ encode the difference between unravelings. This perturbation
is well-controlled near $\zeta = 1$ (the regime where our numerics show the
broadest crossover) and may give cleaner predictions for the "softening"
of the crossover that we observe.

LMR's bosonization is specifically tuned to the strong-PPS regime where the
Ising fixed point governs; a Born-rule perturbation expansion would give
predictions in the opposite regime. The two approaches can be matched in
the middle (around the conjectured $\zeta^*$ of LMR) for a complete picture.

For QJ specifically, a $(1 - \zeta)$ expansion gives a clean structure
because at $\zeta = 1$ the cross-replica term is just the Born-rule click
correlator, which factorises into single-replica click correlators (no
cross-correlation beyond what the Born-rule Lindblad already produces).
The leading $(1 - \zeta)$ correction is then a single-particle correction
to the Luttinger parameter, which should be computable in closed form.

This is, I think, the most tractable analytical project beyond what's in
the present document.
