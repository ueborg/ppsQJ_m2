# Theoretical collaboration prompt — QJ-PPS Case B project

This document is a self-contained briefing for a theoretical physics collaborator
(human or AI) to continue analytical work on the project. All claimed results are
supported by numerics or cited literature; open problems are explicitly labelled.

---

## Model

A 1D quantum jump (QJ) model of $L$ spinless fermions with partial post-selection
(PPS). The Lindbladian is

$$\mathcal{L}\rho = -i[H,\rho] + \alpha\sum_j\!\Bigl(\tilde L_j\,\rho\,\tilde L_j^\dagger - \tfrac{1}{2}\{\tilde L_j^\dagger\tilde L_j,\rho\}\Bigr)$$

with free hopping $H = -w\sum_j(c^\dagger_{j+1}c_j + \text{h.c.})$ and jump
operator $\tilde L_j = d^\dagger_j d_j$ (the density of a Bogoliubov–Kitaev
bond mode $d_j$, not the physical density $c^\dagger_j c_j$). Parametrize
$\alpha + w = 1$, $\lambda\equiv\alpha$.

PPS: each trajectory with $N_T$ quantum jumps receives weight $\zeta^{N_T}$,
$\zeta\in(0,1]$. At $\zeta=1$ this is Born-rule QJ; at $\zeta\to 0$ only the
no-click trajectory survives (the Doob/non-Hermitian limit).

**Symmetry class**: class DIII (established in the replica Keldysh action
framework below, following Le Gal–Schirò arXiv:2511.22506).

## Established results

### A. The Keldysh–replica action

Starting from the $N$-replicated master equation and following Le Gal–Schirò,
the trajectory-averaged $\mathcal{R}^{(N)}_t = \mathbb{E}_{\mathbb{Q}_\zeta}[\rho_t^{\otimes N}]$
is encoded in a path integral with action

$$S[\bar\Phi,\Phi;\zeta] = S_{\rm kin} + S_{\rm nH} - i\zeta\gamma\!\int\!dt\sum_j\prod_{r=1}^N\mathcal{V}_{j,r}$$

where:
- $S_{\rm kin} + S_{\rm nH}$ is replica-diagonal and **independent of $\zeta$**.
- $\prod_r\mathcal{V}_{j,r}$ is the cross-replica vertex; **$\zeta$ appears only here**.
- $\mathcal{V}_{j,r} = \tfrac{1}{4}\exp[2\bar\Phi_{j,r}(\sigma_x\otimes M_j)\Phi_{j,r}]$
  where $M_j = \tau_z + i\sin(k)\tau_y$ is the Nambu structure of $\tilde L_j$.

This is the PPS generalization of Le Gal–Schirò Eqs. (16)–(17). $\zeta$ entering
only in the cross-replica vertex is an exact structural fact, not an approximation.

### B. Hubbard–Stratonovich decoupling and NLSM target

After HS decoupling of $\prod_r\mathcal{V}_{j,r}$ via a matrix field $Q$, the
replica-symmetric Lindbladian saddle $Q_0$ is identified. Fluctuations of $Q$
around $Q_0$ live on the coset

$$\mathcal{G}/\mathcal{H} = SO(N) \quad\text{(class DIII)}$$

where $\mathcal{G} = SO(N)\times SO(N)$ is fixed by the symmetry constraints
from both $H$ and $\tilde L_j$, and $\mathcal{H} = SO(N)_{\rm diag}$ is the
residual symmetry of the saddle. The DIII target $SO(N)$ has $\pi_2 = \mathbb{Z}_2$
for $N\geq 3$.

### C. The matched-NLSM prediction and its inputs

**Two inputs:**
1. **UV dimension of the cross-Choi vertex:**
   $\Delta_\zeta^{\rm UV} = 1$ (engineering dimension of $\prod_r\mathcal{V}_{j,r}$ at free-Dirac UV, confirmed by lattice 2-point function $\langle\mathcal{V}_\zeta(x)\mathcal{V}_\zeta(0)\rangle \sim x^{-2}$).
2. **Multicritical class-DIII NLSM exponent:**
   $\nu = 2$ (König–Brouwer PRB 90, 165140, 2014; Le Gal–Schirò 2025).

**The argument:**
Near the multicritical point $(\lambda,\zeta)=(0,0)$, the linearised RG equations are
$$\frac{d\lambda}{d\ell} = y_\lambda\lambda,\quad \frac{d\zeta}{d\ell} = y_\zeta\zeta,\quad y_\lambda = 1/\nu = 1/2,\quad y_\zeta = 2-\Delta_\zeta^{\rm UV} = 1.$$

Choose the scale $\ell_\lambda$ where $\lambda(\ell_\lambda)\sim 1$:
$$e^{\ell_\lambda}\sim\lambda^{-1/y_\lambda} = \lambda^{-2},\quad\text{so}\quad \xi_\lambda^{\rm cross}\sim\lambda^{-2}\,a.$$

This is the **crossover length** generated by $\lambda$-perturbation of the
multicritical FP — NOT the diverging critical correlation length $\xi_{\rm crit}$
(which would be circular). The running $\zeta$ at this scale:
$$\zeta_{\rm eff}(\xi_\lambda^{\rm cross}) = \zeta\cdot\lambda^{-y_\zeta/y_\lambda} = \zeta\cdot\lambda^{-2}.$$

Criticality when $\zeta_{\rm eff} = K^*$ (universal NLSM critical coupling):
$$\boxed{\lambda_c = \sqrt{c_\lambda/K^*}\,\sqrt{\zeta}\equiv C\sqrt{\zeta},\qquad C = O(1)\text{ non-universal.}}$$

The natural one-parameter interpolation recovering $\lambda_c(1)=1/2$ (Carollo PRA 2018):
$$\lambda_c(\zeta) = \frac{\sqrt{\zeta}}{1+\sqrt{\zeta}}.$$

**Numerical confirmation:**
Global FSS on the cloning data at $L\in\{64,96,128,192,256\}$ across
$\zeta\in[0.03,0.85]$ gives $\phi = 0.56\pm 0.05$ (consistent with $\phi=1/2$
at $1.3\sigma$; $\phi=1$ excluded at $9\sigma$) and $C = 1.02\pm 0.10$.

## Open theoretical problems — in priority order

### Problem 1 (most important): Compute $\Delta_\zeta^{\rm IR}$ at the class-DIII NLSM fixed point

**Why it matters:** The matched-NLSM argument uses $\Delta_\zeta^{\rm UV}=1$
(the UV, free-Dirac dimension) to run $\zeta$ from scale $a$ to $\xi_\lambda^{\rm cross}$.
If the IR fixed point of the DIII NLSM has a *different* scaling dimension
$\Delta_\zeta^{\rm IR}\neq 1$ for the cross-Choi operator, the prediction
$\phi = 1/2$ receives a correction:
$$\phi_{\rm corrected} = \frac{y_\lambda}{y_\zeta^{\rm IR}} = \frac{1/2}{2-\Delta_\zeta^{\rm IR}}.$$

To get $\phi = 1/2$ exactly we need either $\Delta_\zeta^{\rm IR} = 1$ or the
additional RG window $[\xi_\lambda^{\rm cross},\xi_{\rm crit}]$ to be negligible.

**What's needed:** Identify the operator $\mathcal{O}_\zeta^{\rm NLSM}[Q]$ in
the NLSM that corresponds to $\prod_r\mathcal{V}_{j,r}$ after HS
decoupling, i.e., compute

$$\mathcal{O}_\zeta^{\rm NLSM}[Q] = \frac{\partial S_{\rm NLSM}}{\partial\zeta}\bigg|_{\zeta=0}$$

and then compute its one-loop anomalous dimension $\gamma_\zeta$ at the class-DIII
NLSM fixed point (i.e., at the SO(N) NLSM with $N\to 1$). If $\gamma_\zeta = 0$,
then $\Delta_\zeta^{\rm IR} = \Delta_\zeta^{\rm UV} = 1$ and $\phi = 1/2$ is exact.

**What's known:** Le Gal–Schirò arXiv:2511.22506, §5.3.3 contains the
class-DIII NLSM action for the Born-rule ($\zeta=1$) case. The NLSM action is
the $SO(N)$ principal chiral model:
$$S_{\rm NLSM}[Q] = \frac{K}{8\pi}\int d^2x\,\mathrm{Tr}[(\partial_\mu Q)^2] + \text{WZW term}.$$
The bare coupling $K^{-1} \propto g_B \propto \zeta\lambda/(1-\lambda)$.
The $\zeta$-dependent part enters $K$ linearly through the cross-replica vertex.

**Specific calculation requested:**
1. Starting from the HS-decoupled action, take $\partial/\partial\zeta$ at $\zeta=0$
   to identify $\mathcal{O}_\zeta^{\rm NLSM}[Q]$.
2. Classify it: is it a marginal deformation of the stiffness $K$, a WZW coefficient,
   or something else?
3. Compute $\gamma_\zeta$ at one loop using background-field RG on $SO(N)$ NLSM,
   then take $N\to 1$.
4. Does $\gamma_\zeta = 0$? If yes, $\Delta_\zeta^{\rm IR} = 1$ and $\phi = 1/2$ is exact.

### Problem 2 (clarifies the mechanism): Microscopic origin of $\xi_\lambda^{\rm cross}\sim\lambda^{-2}$

**Why it matters:** In the argument above, $\xi_\lambda^{\rm cross}\sim\lambda^{-2}$
is identified via $\nu=2$ at the DIII multicritical FP. This is correct but abstract.
A complementary question: can this scale be seen directly in the microscopic model?

**One proposed mechanism (from a collaborator):** The jump operator
$\tilde L_j = d^\dagger_j d_j$ is a Bogoliubov-mode density, NOT the
physical density $c^\dagger_j c_j$. Because $d$ and $c$ differ by a
Bogoliubov rotation, $[H, \sum_j d^\dagger_j d_j]\neq 0$. This means the
no-click effective Hamiltonian
$$H_{\rm eff} = H - \tfrac{i\alpha}{2}\sum_j d^\dagger_j d_j$$
is NOT just "hopping + uniform imaginary chemical potential" in the $c$-basis.
In Majorana language, $d^\dagger_j d_j = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 - i\gamma_{2j}\gamma_{2j+3})$,
so $H_{\rm eff}$ contains both a uniform norm decay AND a non-Hermitian
Majorana-bond coupling $\sim\alpha\sum_j\gamma_{2j}\gamma_{2j+3}$.

**The SSH-like calculation:** Writing the non-Hermitian Majorana chain in
momentum space with $t_1 = w - i\alpha$, $t_2 = -w$ (the SSH-like bond ratio is
$|r| = |t_2/t_1| = w/\sqrt{w^2+\alpha^2}$), the claim is:
$$\xi_{\rm SSH} = \frac{1}{-\log|r|}\approx\frac{w^2}{\alpha^2}\sim\lambda^{-2}\quad(\lambda\ll 1).$$

**The issue:** Direct diagonalization of $H_{\rm eff}$ as built in the project
code does NOT show this $\lambda^{-2}$ scaling cleanly — the bulk spectral gap
moves in the opposite direction. The model's measured bonds couple Majoranas
$(2j, 2j+3)$ separated by 3 indices, not the standard nearest-neighbor SSH
unit cell. This discrepancy needs resolution.

**What's needed:**
1. Write down $H_{\rm eff}$ explicitly in the Majorana basis
   $\gamma_{2j}$, $\gamma_{2j+1}$ for the lattice model with PBC.
2. Identify the correct unit cell for the non-Hermitian chain (it may be
   larger than 2 sites if the bond structure is non-local in the Majorana index).
3. Compute the momentum-space Hamiltonian $H(k)$ for this unit cell.
4. Find the imaginary part of the lowest-lying bulk eigenvalue: $\Delta(k,\lambda) = |\mathrm{Im}\,E_{\rm min}(k)|$.
5. Determine whether $\xi = v_F/\Delta \sim \lambda^{-2}$ at small $\lambda$.

### Problem 3 (medium term): Closed-form crossover function

The small-$\zeta$ prediction $\lambda_c\sim\sqrt\zeta$ and the large-$\zeta$
endpoint $\lambda_c(1) = 1/2$ are both established. The proposed interpolation
$\lambda_c = \sqrt\zeta/(1+\sqrt\zeta)$ fits the data well, but it is a
phenomenological ansatz, not derived.

**What's needed:** In principle, the crossover function
$\lambda_c(\zeta)$ follows from the RG flow equation for a theory with
two relevant couplings. The flow equations are:
$$\frac{d\lambda}{d\ell} = y_\lambda\lambda + \text{(nonlinear)},\quad
\frac{d\zeta}{d\ell} = y_\zeta\zeta + \text{(nonlinear)}.$$

The critical line is the RG separatrix. For the simple linear problem the
separatrix IS $\lambda_c\propto\sqrt\zeta$. Nonlinear corrections deform it.
To get the full $\lambda_c(\zeta)$ including the $\zeta=1$ endpoint, one would
need to run the RG from the multicritical FP at $(0,0)$ to the Born-rule FP
at $(\lambda_c^{\rm BR}=1/2,\,\zeta=1)$ and find the separatrix.

This is potentially doable from the one-loop $\beta$-functions of the DIII NLSM
with two parameters $(\lambda,\zeta)$, but is secondary to Problems 1 and 2.

## Relevant literature and context

- **Le Gal–Schirò** arXiv:2511.22506: The main field-theory framework; defines the
  Keldysh-replica action, HS decoupling, symmetry analysis, and Born-rule NLSM
  for class D (Case A, two measurements) and class DIII (Case B). All NLSM
  structural claims in this project follow from their machinery.
- **König–Brouwer** PRB 90, 165140 (2014): Establishes $\nu=2$ for the class-DIII
  multicritical NLSM (in the disordered-fermion context; transcribed to MIPT here).
- **Carollo et al.** PRA 98, 010103 (2018): Born-rule ($\zeta=1$) endpoint
  $\lambda_c = 1/2$.
- **Kells–Meidan–Romito** SciPost Phys. 14, 031 (2023): The original quantum-diffusion
  analogue, including the $\xi_{\rm ps}\sim\lambda^{-2}$ scaling for the related
  QSD model. The exact relation to the QJ-PPS Case B scaling is the substance of
  Problem 2 above.
